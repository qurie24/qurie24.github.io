Bristol

Direct your steps now in front of the famous Bristol Hotel.

You are standing before the Bristol Hotel, one of the most prestigious hotels in Warsaw. Its walls have witnessed countless stories—this is where famous politicians, artists, and even royalty have stayed. In 1913, the Malinowa Hall hosted a banquet in honor of Maria Skłodowska-Curie, who by then had won two Nobel Prizes. Interestingly, during the speeches, instead of basking in the praise, Maria was focused on solving mathematical problems.

Task: Enter through the Cafe Bristol entrance (the very corner of the building). Just to the left behind the door, you'll find a large Fibonacci spiral made of decorative door handles. Locate the handle with Maria's name, and right next to it, the handle of a famous pianist. What is his name?

Hint: The earlier visitors, the closer to the center they are.
