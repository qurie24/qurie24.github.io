Now, follow the flow of the river, walking along the Vistula Boulevards. This beautiful stretch along the river connects Warsaw to its historic heart. The Vistula has played an essential role in the city's development—it's more than just a river; it's a living part of Warsaw's story.

According to legend, the name Wisła (Vistula) comes from Wars and Sawa, the mythical founders of Warsaw. Here's how it goes:

Legend of Wars and Sawa:

Long ago, a humble fisherman named Wars lived in a simple cottage by the river's edge. Every evening, he would sit by the Vistula, mesmerized by its waters, when one night he heard a mysterious, enchanting song. Driven by curiosity, Wars set out in his boat toward the source of the voice. Suddenly, the river surged, and fierce waves threatened to capsize him. Lightning cracked the sky, breaking his oars, and winds tore at his fishing nets—but Wars pressed on. In the heart of the storm, he saw her: a beautiful siren with a shimmering silver tail. Her name was Sawa.

Reaching out, Wars offered her his hand. Sawa transformed into a woman and gave him a sword and shield, entrusting him with her protection, as well as that of the river and the city that would one day bear their names—Warszawa.
